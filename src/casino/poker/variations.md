# Poker Variations

## Texas Hold'em

The most popular poker variant. Each player receives 2 hole cards, and 5 community cards are dealt in the center.

### Game Flow

1. **Preflop**: Each player receives 2 hole cards. Betting starts with players to the left of the big blind.
2. **Flop**: 3 community cards are dealt. Another betting round.
3. **Turn**: 1 more community card is dealt. Another betting round.
4. **River**: Final community card is dealt. Final betting round.
5. **Showdown**: Remaining players show their best 5-card hand using any combination of hole cards and community cards.

### Betting Structures

- **No-Limit**: Any bet up to all your chips
- **Pot-Limit**: Maximum bet is the current pot size
- **Fixed-Limit**: Fixed bet sizes, typically doubles on the river

## Omaha

Similar to Hold'em but each player receives 4 hole cards. At showdown, players MUST use exactly 2 of their hole cards combined with exactly 3 community cards.

### Game Flow

Same as Texas Hold'em: preflop → flop → turn → river → showdown

### Omaha Hi-Lo

Split pot variant where the lowest hand (8 or better) wins half the pot. Ace-to-five lowball ranking is used for low hands.

## 7-Card Stud

Classic poker variant with no community cards. Each player can have up to 7 cards total.

### Game Flow

1. **Third Street**: Each player receives 2 down cards and 1 up card. Betting starts with the lowest up card.
2. **Fourth Street**: Each player receives 1 up card. Betting starts with lowest hand showing.
3. **Fifth Street**: Each player receives 1 up card. Betting starts with lowest hand showing.
4. **Sixth Street**: Each player receives 1 up card. Betting starts with lowest hand showing.
5. **Seventh Street**: Each player receives 1 down card. Final betting round.
6. **Showdown**: Remaining players show their best 5-card hand from their 7 cards.

### Betting

Typically fixed-limit, with limits doubling on sixth street (or "river" equivalent).
